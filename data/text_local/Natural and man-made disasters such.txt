Natural and man-made disasters such as floods, earthquakes, wildfires, and landslides pose serious threats to human life, infrastructure, and the environment. Existing disaster detection and response systems largely depend on single-source information such as sensor readings or manual reporting, which often results in delayed alerts, inaccurate predictions, and inefficient emergency response. To address these limitations, this project presents an AI-Driven Multimodal Disaster Detection and Response System that leverages Artificial Intelligence to analyze heterogeneous data sources in an integrated manner. The proposed system combines image data, textual information, and environmental sensor data to achieve accurate and timely disaster detection. Deep Learning models, particularly Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), are employed to analyze visual data obtained from satellite imagery, drones, and surveillance cameras for identifying disaster events such as floods and fires. Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques are used to analyze text data from social media posts, emergency alerts, and news reports to extract disaster-related information and assess urgency levels. In addition, Machine Learning models process structured sensor data such as temperature, rainfall, humidity, and seismic readings to predict potential disaster risks.The outputs generated from the individual models are integrated using a multimodal fusion approach, which produces a unified Disaster Risk Score representing the severity and likelihood of a disaster. Based on this risk score, the system generates real-time alerts and provides decision support to authorities for effective disaster mitigation, evacuation planning, and rescue operations. The proposed solution reduces false alarms, improves situational awareness, and enhances response efficiency. Overall, the system offers a scalable, software-based solution that can support smart city initiatives and large-scale disaster management systems.
